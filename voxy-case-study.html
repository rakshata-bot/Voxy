How it ran

The testing loop.

Every week: new batch out, then read the board on Monday. Cost, CTR, install-to-trial, CAC. Whatever won got more budget and got re-cut into new variants. Whatever lost got killed quickly. One ad on its own brought in 28,000+ trials.

The winner sheet basically ran the team. It decided what we briefed next and where the money went.

Spending more, paying less per user.

Buying growth is easy if you just throw money at it. The question is whether it holds up. We pushed weekly spend about 2.7× and CAC still came down ~14%, ₹56 to ₹48. That drop is the creative working, not the budget.

Where I drew the line

The cheap lever I didn't pull.

Voxy sells to people who feel alone. The easiest way to grow that is to twist the screw: make the ads sound desperate, imply the app fixes you. I didn't run those. The hooks stayed relatable and the claims stayed true to what it is, which is an app, not a cure.

It didn't cost us anything to hold that line. The numbers up there came off ads I'd still put my name on.

Started with a 3am text nobody answers. Ended with 601K installs, 193K trials, and a CAC that was lower than when we were spending less.
